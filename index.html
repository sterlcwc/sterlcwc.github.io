Bio

I'm a PhD Student in Information Science at Cornell University, where I am extremely fortunate to be co-advised by Professor Michael Macy and Professor Mor Naaman. My research interests lie in testing the replicability and applicability of insights from social psychology, communication, and the behavioral sciences more broadly to new, rapidly-emerging, and unfamiliar online environments. As two recent examples of this work, I have a paper in progress that looks at how people's judgments of the trustworthiness of health information are potentially biased by the way the information is visually presented in the form of Google search results. As an undergraduate student, I was the lead author on a paper testing the efficacy of video interventions to reduce parents' willingness to overshare about their children online, which appeared in the SRCD Conference with a top 1% award in Interdisciplinary Research, and in the journal Computers in Human Behavior in 2021 (read here).

I received my B.A. in Social Psychology along with a B.A. in the Robert S. Harrison College Scholar program, with formal minors in Information Science and Communication. I was honored as a Summa cum Laude graduate, a Merrill Presidential Scholar (the top 1% of my graduating class) and a Phi Beta Kappa inductee. In my graduate studies, I have been fortunate to receive exceptional mentorship from both of my coadvisors and many other professors along the way, which I enjoy paying forward by mentoring other students spanning high schoolers to other PhD students. I aspire to become a professor after finishing my PhD. As a hobby, I enjoy outdoor running and learning new coding languages (my favorite is R).
