Bio

I'm a PhD Student in Information Science at Cornell University, and I am extremely fortunate to be co-advised by Professor Michael Macy and Professor Mor Naaman. My research interests lie in testing the replicability and applicability of insights from social psychology, communication, and the behavioral sciences more broadly to new, rapidly-emerging, novel, and unfamiliar online environments. As two recent examples of this work, I have a paper in progress that looks at how humans' judgments of the trustworthiness of health information are potentially biased by the way the information is presented in Google search results, namely by the rank of the results, the presence of misinformation, and the presence of warning banners about unreliable information. As an undergraduate student, I was the lead author on a paper tetsing the efficacy of video interventions to reduce parents' willingness to overshare about their children online, which appeared in the SRCD Conference with an award in Interdisciplinary Research and in the journal Computers in Human Behavior .

I received my B.A. in Social Psychology along with a B.A. in the Robert S. Harrison College Scholar program, Summa cum Laude, with formal minors in Information Science and Communication. I was honored as a Merrill Presidential Scholar (the top 1% of my graduating class) and a Phi Beta Kappa inductee. In my graduate studies, I have been fortunate to receive exceptional mentorship from both of my coadvisors and many other professors along the way, which I enjoy paying forward by mentoring other students both inside and outside my PhD program. I aspire to become a professor at a research-intensive university after finishing my PhD.
